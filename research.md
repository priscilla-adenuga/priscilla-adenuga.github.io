# Research

## 🧠 Research Focus

My research sits at the intersection of theoretical linguistics and Natural Language Processing (NLP), with a particular focus on how linguistic structure shapes interpretation and how these insights can improve the evaluation of computational language systems.

I am especially interested in structurally complex phenomena such as negation, ambiguity, modifier structure, and underspecification, and in what model behavior on these phenomena reveals about the strengths and limitations of current NLP systems.

My work also explores how low-resource and underrepresented languages can function as diagnostic tools for evaluating robustness, representation, and generalization in multilingual NLP.

## 📄 Ongoing Work

### Morpho-Syntactically Annotated Corpus of Ògè
Development of a linguistically grounded annotated corpus of Ògè folk narratives with a focus on nominal structure, modification, and interpretation in low-resource settings.

### Syntax-Informed Negation in NLP
Exploring how different types of negation (e.g. verbal, adjectival, and discourse-level negation) affect model interpretation and evaluation outcomes.

### Low-Resource Languages as Stress Tests for NLP
Investigating how structurally diverse and underrepresented languages reveal hidden weaknesses in NLP systems, particularly in annotation practices, ambiguity resolution, and evaluation design.

### Linguistically Informed NLP Evaluation
Developing evaluation approaches that incorporate linguistic structure and interpretation, moving beyond surface-level metrics to better capture model behavior and meaning representation.
